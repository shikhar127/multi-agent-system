How it works

Three roles.
Structured debate.

The classifier routes each question to the right workflow — 2 to 4 rounds of debate — then synthesizes a final answer with confidence and dissent.

Solver
Builds & proposes

Constructs the primary answer or solution. Takes a definitive position and explains its reasoning step by step.

→
Challenger
Attacks & stress-tests

Finds flaws, edge cases, and faulty assumptions. Plays devil's advocate to force a stronger answer.

→
Scout
Finds alternatives

Explores reframings, alternative approaches, and perspectives the Solver missed. Prevents tunnel vision.

→
Synthesizer
Final verdict

Integrates all rounds into a confident final answer with confidence rating, dissent, and full trace.

Workflows

Four question types.
Four debate strategies.

The classifier detects question type automatically and routes it to the optimal deliberation workflow.

A — Factual2–3 rounds
Multi-Agent Debate + Cross-Verification

All three models answer independently, then compare and argue. Arbitration fires only if Round 2 doesn't converge.

e.g. "What is the capital of France?"
B — Analytical3–4 rounds
Dialectical Debate + Devil's Advocate

Solver builds a full solution; Challenger and Scout attack it; Solver revises; then all converge on a final answer.

e.g. "Rewrite in Go or migrate from Python?"
C — Creative4 rounds
Nominal Group + Adversarial Collaboration

Each model takes a different persona — Pragmatic, Contrarian, Ambitious — then cross-pollinates into a hybrid recommendation.

e.g. "Design a rate-limiting strategy"
D — Judgment4 rounds
ACH Evidence Matrix + Crux Analysis

Models take distinct positions, build an evidence matrix, identify crux disagreements, then issue a final judgment with reversal conditions.

e.g. "Microservices vs monolith?"
