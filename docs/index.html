    ROUND 01
    SOLVER
    "Prosperity. The Data Is Clear."

    Global extreme poverty dropped from 36% in 1990 to under 10% today. Life expectancy, literacy, caloric intake — all up. Capitalism isn't perfect, but no system has lifted more people faster.

    POSITION: NET POSITIVE
    CHALLENGER
    "Those Numbers Hide the Cost."

    The top 1% now own more wealth than the bottom 50% combined. Poverty fell — but so did labour's share of GDP in every major economy. Prosperity for whom, exactly?

    POSITION: STRUCTURAL EXPLOITATION
    SCOUT
    "The Answer Depends on the Rules."

    Nordic capitalism outperforms US capitalism on equality and mobility. The variable isn't capitalism itself — it's regulation, redistribution, and labour rights. Same system, wildly different outcomes.

    POSITION: CONTEXT-DEPENDENT
    CLASH!!ROUND 02 — CHALLENGE & COUNTERCLASH!!
    ROUND 02
    CHALLENGER → SOLVER
    "Correlation Isn't Causation."

    Poverty fell alongside capitalism — but also alongside antibiotics, the green revolution, and post-war institutions. You can't credit capitalism for penicillin. The counterfactual isn't a free market; it's a better-regulated one.

    FLAW: CAUSATION UNPROVEN
    SCOUT — EVIDENCE
    "Denmark Breaks the Binary."

    Denmark scores top 3 globally on both economic freedom and social equality. It runs on capitalism with strong unions and a 46% tax rate. The prosperity-vs-exploitation framing is a false choice — the dial is regulation, not the system.

    EVIDENCE: REGULATION IS THE VARIABLE
    CONVERGENCE!!ROUND 03 — FINAL POSITIONCONVERGENCE!!
    ROUND 03
    SOLVER — REVISED
    "Both Are True. The Question Is Which Wins."

    Capitalism does generate prosperity and exploitation simultaneously. The Scout's evidence is decisive: the dial is regulation. Unregulated capitalism concentrates power and extracts labour. Regulated capitalism with strong redistributive policy delivers broad prosperity. The original question is the wrong frame.

    REVISED: DEPENDS ON REGULATION
    CHALLENGER
    "Conceded on Framing."

    The binary was always false. I concur: the relevant question is what constraints capitalism operates under, not whether to have it at all.

    CONVERGENCE ACHIEVED
    — FINAL JUDGMENT —

    ⬡ HIGH CONFIDENCE

    Both — but the framing is wrong. Capitalism produces prosperity or exploitation depending on how tightly it is regulated. The question to ask is not "capitalism yes or no" but "what rules does it operate under." Denmark proves broad prosperity is achievable; unregulated markets prove extraction is the default.
